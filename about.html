About

I'm a photographer based in Berlin, Germany specialising in photographing landscape and architecture. I also have a passion for Web Development.

Photography

I was born in the south of Ukraine, a place where Art Nouveau meets Social Realism.

Growing up here and studying environmental science, I met people who introduced me to the realm of natural beauty and art. This was the time when I first encountered the desire to be a photographer. Nonetheless, overwhelmed by my university routine, I let go of the idea to engage in photography.

After finishing my master's degree, I moved to Berlin, with a plan to pursue a career in environmental protection. Having lived in this city, and being inspired by the high volume of art and diverse architecture, I returned to my passion for capturing pictures. This time I was determined to embrace it and develop this interest into a valuable skill.

In the last few years, I have learned a large variety of techniques in landscape and architectural photography. Along with this, I have developed excellent skills for editing images in photoshop.

My work has been featured on BBC website and in a book publication for Cambridge Scholars Publishing Ltd.

Web Development

One of my latest tech-infatuations involves programming and web development. And, to break into this almost enigmatic discipline, I have recently been ambitiously engaging in learning HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

Having spent almost a year studying, I had decided to consolidate my new knowledge by putting it into practice and building a portfolio that would not only show my fresh skills, but also help me cement my interest in this field.

To a certain extend, this website signifies the Aha! Moment in my learning progress. Following are the tools and means that helped me create it:

  • Most of the code was hand-written in Visual Studio Code software;
  • I structured the front page in HTML with the help of Bootstrap and using my own code;
  • I took advantage of CSS Grid for the landing page as well as for the image galleries;
  • As an engine behind all hover and click events I utilised Vanilla JavaScript;
  • Adobe Fonts & Font Awesome (cos it really is!) helped embellish the visual side of things;
